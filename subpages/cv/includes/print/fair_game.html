The fair game paradox:

Adam and Bolek play a game:

An automaton generate two consecutive natural numbers and randomly gives each of them to Adam and Bolek.
Adam knows the number of Bolek and Bolek knows number of Adam, but they don't know their own numbers.
The person with smaller number pays the other as many dollars as value assigned to him.
Each of players can resign at any moment and/or ask for an another pair of numbers.

But nobody will resign or ask for a new numbers, since each of them is reasoning:

"Let my opponent has a value k, then I have value (k-1) or (k+1) with equal probability."
"If I will win, I get k+1, if I will lose I lose k-1, so on average I gain a 50 cents."

The second player thinks the same way, so game is a good business for both of them.

Where is the error in theirs logic.
